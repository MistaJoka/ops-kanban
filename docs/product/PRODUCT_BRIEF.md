# Product Brief

## Product name

Working name: **OpsBoard AI**

## Product category

AI-first SMB Operational Command Center.

## Target user

**Landscaping / lawn-care SMB** — owner, office manager, or lead installer with a small crew. Needs one system for inquiries, site visits, estimates, crew scheduling, invoices, and follow-ups across residential and light commercial properties.

Vertical details: `VERTICAL_LANDSCAPING.md`. MVP scope: `MVP_SCOPE.md`.

## Core problem

Small businesses operate through fragmented tools:

- texts
- calls
- paper notes
- spreadsheets
- calendars
- payment apps
- disconnected CRMs
- memory

This creates missed follow-ups, slow payments, unclear job status, weak accountability, and poor visibility.

## Core solution

One universal operations board where each card represents a business lifecycle object.

**Platform vision:** Best-of-SaaS capabilities (scheduling, e-sign, comms, payments, documents, accounting) **scaled down and attached to each job card**—not a dozen disconnected subscriptions. Roadmap: `PLATFORM_CAPABILITIES.md`.

A card can contain:

- lead details
- customer info
- job scope
- quote / e-sign status
- schedule (visit book + crew dates)
- invoice + **payment link** (PayPal/Stripe)
- SMS/email thread
- files (photos, signed PDFs)
- comments
- activity log
- AI summary
- next action

## Product philosophy

The app should avoid splitting the business across many boards. One main board is the central workspace. Other pages are views and lenses over the same operational data.

## Main promise

Help a business move every opportunity from first contact to paid and archived with fewer missed steps.

## MVP success definition

The MVP succeeds when a user can:

1. Create a lead.
2. Move the lead through the pipeline.
3. Store customer and job data on the card.
4. Draft a quote.
5. Schedule the work.
6. Mark work complete.
7. Create an invoice.
8. Track payment.
9. Archive the record.
10. Ask AI to help update, analyze, summarize, and prepare actions.
